# Report Style Guide

This guide defines the language standards for generated AI Visibility / GEO Audit reports.

The goal is to make reports professional, cautious, commercially useful, and suitable for consulting-style diagnostic work.

---

## Core Principle

Reports must describe results as **AI visibility benchmark signals**, not as definitive market facts.

The tool evaluates how brands appear in AI-generated answers within a tested prompt set. It does not measure real market share, sales, consumer preference, clinical quality, or business performance.

---

## Preferred Language

Use language that clearly ties findings to the benchmark context.

Preferred phrases:

- "Within the tested prompt set..."
- "The benchmark indicates..."
- "The generated answers suggest..."
- "The observed visibility signal..."
- "The target brand was not visible in this benchmark run..."
- "This indicates an opportunity to build AI-citable evidence..."
- "This should be validated in future benchmark cycles..."
- "This is a diagnostic signal, not a definitive market conclusion."

---

## Phrases to Avoid

Avoid language that overstates certainty, causality, or business impact.

Do not use phrases such as:

- "proves"
- "guarantees"
- "will increase"
- "will improve rankings"
- "will drive revenue"
- "will increase sales"
- "consumers prefer"
- "market leader"
- "best brand"
- "dominates the market"
- "AI knows"
- "AI trusts"
- "clinically proven" unless externally validated and provided by the user

---

## Benchmark-Safe Interpretation

When describing results, use measured benchmark language.

Use:

- "higher measured visibility"
- "stronger benchmark signal"
- "more frequent mentions in generated answers"
- "greater share of voice within the tested prompt set"
- "limited measurable visibility in this benchmark"

Avoid:

- "better brand"
- "stronger product"
- "more trusted by consumers"
- "higher market demand"
- "superior clinical performance"

---

## Recommendations

Recommendations should be framed as actions that may improve future benchmark visibility.

Use:

- "intended to improve AI-citable evidence"
- "designed to strengthen target-brand association"
- "recommended for future benchmark validation"
- "may improve visibility in future AI-generated answers"

Avoid:

- "will improve AI rankings"
- "will increase share of voice"
- "guarantees more mentions"
- "will drive sales"
- "will improve conversion"

---

## Quick Test Mode Language

Quick Test Mode is for development and QA only.

Required meaning:

- Quick Test Mode is not client-deliverable.
- Results are based on a limited prompt set.
- Findings are directional and should not be treated as a complete benchmark.

Approved wording:

- "TEST VERSION ONLY"
- "Quick Test Mode - Not Client Deliverable"
- "Development-only limited-prompt output"
- "This output is intended for workflow testing and QA."

Avoid in Quick Test Mode:

- strong competitive conclusions
- exact future numeric targets
- client-ready recommendations
- market-wide conclusions

---

## Brand Intelligence Language

Brand Intelligence sections are diagnostic. They may include AI-inferred signals, competitor associations, and market interpretation, but they are not part of visibility scoring unless explicitly included in the tracked competitor set.

Required distinction:

- Tracked competitors are included in scoring and share of voice.
- AI-discovered brands and market signals are diagnostic references only.
- Diagnostic inferences should be validated before being used as client-facing facts.

Approved wording:

- "Diagnostic inference"
- "AI-inferred"
- "Not included in visibility scoring"
- "Requires validation before external use"
- "Observed as a positioning signal, not as measured market evidence"

---

## Methodology and Limitations

Reports must preserve methodology limitations.

Required ideas:

- The report is an AI visibility benchmark.
- It is not a market share report.
- It is not a sales performance report.
- It is not a consumer survey.
- It is not a clinical or product efficacy evaluation.
- Results may vary between model runs and should be tracked over time.

---

## Client-Facing Tone

The tone should be:

- analytical
- cautious
- specific
- evidence-oriented
- commercially useful
- consulting-style

The tone should not be:

- promotional
- absolute
- speculative without labels
- overly technical
- overly generic
