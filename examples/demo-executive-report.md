# Demo Executive Report: Example Brand AI Visibility Audit

> This is an anonymized demonstration report for portfolio review.  
> It does not represent a real client engagement or confidential brand data.

---

## Executive Summary

This demo audit evaluates how **Example Brand** appears in AI-generated recommendations for **skincare products** in the **Hong Kong** market.

The analysis compares Example Brand against a tracked competitor set and reviews how often the brand appears in AI-generated recommendation, comparison, and decision-stage answers.

The simulated audit shows that Example Brand has moderate visibility in general skincare prompts, but weaker visibility in high-intent recommendation and comparison prompts. Competitors appear more frequently when AI answers emphasize dermatologist credibility, sensitive-skin claims, product availability, and evidence-based trust signals.

### Interpretation

Example Brand appears in some broad category-level answers, but it is not consistently included when AI systems provide ranked recommendations or shortlist-style buying advice.

The main visibility gap is not basic brand recognition. The issue is that AI-generated answers currently associate competitors more strongly with clear product benefits, trust signals, and evidence-backed positioning.

---

## Trigger-Level Visibility Findings

### Stronger Visibility Triggers

Example Brand performs better in prompts related to:

- Niche skincare categories
- Premium skincare interest
- Product discovery for specific skin concerns
- Audience-specific skincare exploration

### Weaker Visibility Triggers

Example Brand performs weaker in prompts related to:

- “Best skincare brands in Hong Kong”
- “Most trusted skincare products”
- “Dermatologist-recommended skincare”
- “Sensitive skin product recommendations”
- “Compare top skincare brands”

### Interpretation

AI systems appear to favor brands with clearer external validation signals, stronger category ownership, and more repeated evidence across public-facing content.

---

## Brand Intelligence Findings

The audit identifies several positioning gaps:

| Finding | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Weak category ownership | Example Brand is not consistently associated with a clear skincare use case. |
| Limited trust signals | Competitors appear more often with dermatologist, clinical, or review-based credibility. |
| Inconsistent recommendation context | The brand appears in discovery-style answers but less often in final recommendation lists. |
| Missing comparison content | AI answers have limited structured evidence for comparing Example Brand against competitors. |

## Strategic Priorities

1. **Strengthen category ownership**  
   Build content that repeatedly connects Example Brand with specific skincare use cases and audience needs.

2. **Increase trust signals**  
   Add structured evidence such as expert commentary, ingredient explanations, customer proof points, and usage guidance.

3. **Improve comparison readiness**  
   Create content that helps AI systems compare Example Brand against known competitors using clear criteria.

4. **Target high-intent prompts**  
   Focus on prompts where users ask for recommendations, comparisons, and purchase decision support.

5. **Measure changes over time**  
   Export benchmark snapshots after content updates to compare visibility improvements across audit runs.
